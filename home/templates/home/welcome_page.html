Teach Inmates and Parolees to Code.

Find them jobs. Watch them succeed. National data shows recidivism rates are highest among those who are unemployed. Criminal justice involved men and women have long struggled to find employment upon release. They have an immediate disadvantage because of their criminal justice involvement, and must compete with non-offenders for unskilled, low-paying jobs. Even when they are successful at finding a job, keeping it is another challenge. As a result, many are unable to comply with the conditions of parole and end up back in prison.

Persevere is an established, nationwide 501(c) 3 non-profit organization designed to assist justice-involved individuals find and maintain employment in high-demand, future-facing industries. We provide a variety of services ranging from coding instruction, software development skills, entrepreneurship, job acquisition, job retention, life skills support, and mentorship.
